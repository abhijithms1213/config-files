### **2. Micro-reminders in everyday life**

- Sticky notes on her desk / laptop: _“Try first, fix later.”_
    
- Phone wallpaper with the mantra.
    
- Quick verbal nudge before she explains something: _“Just try, don’t worry about being perfect.”_

- “*Effort first, perfection later*”
### **3. Make it positive, not judgmental**

- Instead of “Don’t freeze / don’t be afraid”, say:
    
    - “It’s okay to make mistakes—just start.”
        
    - “Even small steps count.”
        
- Focus on _encouragement_ rather than pointing out the problem.
---
If she’s explaining and suddenly doubts herself, instead of going silent, she can say:

- _“I’m not 100% sure, but I think it works like this…”_
    
- _“Let me try to explain in my own way.”_
---
- After the micro-challenge, she writes or says:
    
    - _“Today I tried this…”_
        
    - _“What went well?”_
        
    - _“What I can improve tomorrow?”_
        
- Focus: celebrate effort, not perfection.
